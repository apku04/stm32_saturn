# Orchestration Log: kotov-i2c2-pins

**Timestamp:** 2026-04-23T14:50:20Z  
**Agent:** Kotov (PCB Expert)  
**Task:** I2C2 / INA219 PCB net tracing

## Summary

Kotov decoded the EasyEDA SQLite DB (stm32_lora.eprj) and traced physical PCB connections:
- **INA219 pins:** Physically wired to PB8 (SCL) and PB9 (SDA)
- **INA219 address:** 0x40 (A0/A1 both tied to GND)
- **Separate I2C connector:** PB6/PB7 (external header, not INA219)
- **STM32U073 AF mapping:** PB8/PB9 support AF4→I2C1 or AF6→I2C2

## Finding

The root cause was not the pins (PB8/PB9 are correct), but the alternate function:
- AF4 routes pins to I2C1 peripheral
- AF6 routes pins to I2C2 peripheral
- Current firmware used AF4 (wrong peripheral), must use AF6 for I2C2

## Decision Impact

- **Kotov was correct** on all technical points (pins, AF, address)
- Downstream agents (Yarrick, Eisenhorn, Cawl) used this finding to resolve the I2C2 crisis
- Knowledge shared with Yarrick and Ravenor for understanding the PCB topology
